Troubleshooting Basics

In this section we'll look at simple methods for identifying and solving network issues



Common Networking Problems

No Wi-Fi Connection

One of the most common issues you'll come accross while trying to access the internet is not being able to actually connect to the internet. More often than not this is a hardware issue that can be resolved by checking your router and ensuring devices are connected. Restarting your router may also help!

Slow Internet

This can be an indicator that there are too many devices on one network trying to access the internet. Think about when you are waiting for a package to arrive at your house but so is everyone else in your family. That package will get there eventually, but it might take the delivery service some time to organize the delivery. It's the same with data on the internet, and some times there is what's called "packet loss" and the data never reaches you. This is just like when your package gets lost in real life and doesn't get delivered.
